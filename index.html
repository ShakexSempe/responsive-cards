Ford_Focus

The Focus RS is a performance-oriented version of the Focus hatchback with its flashy exterior, all-wheel-drive and powerful 257 kW engine that produces 440 Nm of torque.

Audi_A3

The Audi RS 3 will impress you with the combination of 5-cylinder power and the characteristic RS features. The 2.5-litre TFSI engine with 294 kW catapults the Sedan from 0 to 100 km/h in just 4.1 seconds.

Renault_Megane

The last few generations of Megane RS have really challenged the perceptions of what compact-sized hot hatchbacks, let alone front-wheel-drive cars, can achieve dynamically. It has a 1.8-litre engine, 4-cylinder turbopetrol engine and has Power/Torque output of 205 kW/390 Nm

Toyota_Yaris

The GR (Gazoo Racing) Yaris is a performance-oriented variant of the Yaris which has the world's most powerful three-cylinder engine installed in a road car. The 1.6-litre turbocharged engine offers up 192 kW and 360 Nm.

Mercedes_A_Class

The four-wheel drive A45 AMG is staggeringly fast in a straight-line with a power output of 280 kW at 6,000 and 475 Nm of torque at 2,250–5,000.

BMW_1_Series

BMW proudly bucks the trend of 4-cylinder motors by offering the M140i a wholesome 6-cylinder unit A nice, round and substantial power output of 250 kW and 500 Nm of torque

Volkswagen_Golf

The most powerful production Golf to date. The turbocharged 2,0-litre TSI engine offers an overall 228 kW with 380 N.m of torque available between 1 850 and 5 300 r/min. Mated with an updated seven-speed DSG transmission, the new R is able to reach 100 km/h, from 0 in a claimed 4,6 seconds

Honda_Civic

"Quite simply the finest hot hatchback to come to market since the VW Golf GTI Clubsport S and Renault Sport Mégane R26.R." The turbocharged 2,0-litre, four-cylinder petrol engine still delivers 228 kW and 400 N.m to the front axle.

Hyundai_i30

The i30 N was developed to demonstrate that Hyundai can indeed produce an exciting performance car that stands in stark contrast to the rest of its more "ordinary" car offerings. Its MVP is a potent 202 kW/353 Nm 2.0-litre turbopetrol engine mated with a 6-speed manual gearbox and an electronically controlled limited-slip diff.
